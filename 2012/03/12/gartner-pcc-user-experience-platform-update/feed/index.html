Though Centralpoint supports authentication with other systems, including LDAP/AD, we still believe the Members module is necessary. Think about this ‘ There are several different members to your site. Take for instance existing clients. You may want to authenticate existing clients with your client database through Global Login. But what about prospective clients? Those users, who are not actually clients yet, may want to sign up for newsletters or online benefits. Here, Centralpoint Members enable you to store their information until they become bona fide clients. In other scenarios, we may authenticate against LDAP/AD for your intranet, which allows us to welcome those users by Role and name, but where would we store their email newsletter preferences, for example? Centralpoint Members allow you to store information about your users safely and securely, while enabling you to serve each user in a personalized manner. I’m happy to share this information with users that are looking for SharePoint alternatives.
